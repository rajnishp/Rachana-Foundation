Become a Member

Become a member of the Rachana Foundation. As a member you can be an instrument of change and influence; by making regional, national and international representation and help shape Intellectual Property policies globally.

Who can become member?

Individual members, Associations, Organisations, Institutions, Business groups, Companies, Inventors, Scientists, IP owners, Entrepreneurs and Small and Medium-Sized Enterprises, Individuals and Societies engaged in Copyright activities, Producers of GI goods, ADR groups etc who are interested in promoting innovation and creativity can become members of the Organisation.

How do I become a member?

For all your membership needs please e-mail us at outreach@rachanafoundation.com

We will be happy to hear from you!

Internship in India

We offer internship programmes throughout the year (limited seats only). Please contact our office for your registration.

Our interns will be treated as part of the team. They help with all sorts of things from administration to research supporting our programme work.

Anyone interested in applying for work experience or internship should send a CV and short cover letter outlining the kind of work they would like to be considered for, to our outreach manager at outreach@rachanafoundation.com

Contribute

If you believe in our cause and wish to support it, please do consider making a donation that will be utilized to further our mission and achieve our aim.

How much do I have to donate?

There is no minimum or maximum limit to the donation you can make, with so much to do. For your security please do not send credit card information via e-mail.

How is my donation used?

All the funding raised by Rachana Foundation is spent on legal research, public information and education by conducting workshops, seminars and campaigns. A small portion is spent on fundraising costs, management and general administration.

About Us

Rachana Foundation is an independent non-profit organization registered under Societies Registration Act and Bombay Public Trust Act. It is based in Mumbai, India. It was conceived by Vasundhara Rao with a mission of raising Intellectual Property awareness in various sections of the society. Vasundhara Rao is a veteran Indian Lawyer with an expertise in Intellectual Property Law and a passion for social work.
